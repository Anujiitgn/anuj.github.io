01 — About

About Me

I am a Ph.D. scholar at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar, working under the supervision of Prof. Vimal Mishra at the Water and Climate Lab. As an Earth Scientist and Hydrologist, my research lies at the intersection of multi-hydrological modelling, remote sensing & GIS, compound & extreme events, and climate change.

My current work focuses on quantifying the human footprint — specifically intensive irrigation, groundwater abstraction, and reservoir operations — on the terrestrial water cycle using robust multi-model frameworks including VIC, CLM, Noah-MP, CWatM, and H08, integrated with satellite observations.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a Geoinformatics Engineer at the Space Applications Centre (SAC-ISRO), contributing to the National Wetland Inventory & Assessment (NWIA-II). I have also contributed to high-resolution hydroclimatic datasets including the Drought Atlas of India and the INDmet precipitation and temperature products.

02 — Focus Areas

Research Interests

🌊

Multi-Hydrological Modelling

Employing VIC, CLM, Noah-MP, CWatM, and H08 for large-scale water budget assessment and model inter-comparison across Indian river basins.

🌡️

Hydrology & Climate Change

Assessing the impact of warming scenarios and changing precipitation patterns on the Indian terrestrial water cycle and river streamflow.

⚡

Compound & Extreme Events

Analysing co-occurring droughts and floods, their land–atmospheric drivers, and cascading hydrological implications at basin scale.

🛰️

Remote Sensing & GIS

Integrating multi-source satellite data (MODIS, scatterometers) with ground observations for water-level estimation and wetland assessment.
